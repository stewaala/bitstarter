The 'Intensive Arabic Language Programme' (IALP) combines the best of traditional Arabic teaching techniques with the latest in language teaching research and artificial intelligence

There are no shortcuts in language learning, but through he systematic application of these proven techniques and conscientious study we are absolutely confident that students of all abilities can achieve proficiency in Arabic within one year

In order to learn a new language students must master the grammar of the language and have an extensive active vocabulary Arabic is a semitic language and the grammar is very different to English. This presents a significant challenge to new students. Most courses try and simplify the grammar, but ironically this generally ends up confusing the student by omitting important details

Traditional Arabic teaching techniques are generally more rigorous in their approach, but don't benefit from the gains in efficiency that can be achieved with the appropriate application of technology and often have a religious motivation

IALP combines the best of the traditional teaching approach with the best of computer based learning in one comprehensive programme

In order to reach an advanced level in Arabic students need to have an active vocabularly of around 5,000 words. Unfortunately there is no shortcut to vocabularly aquisition, but through repition and targetted recall, this target is easily achievable within a year

FAQ

Why does the programme insist on going through the details of Arabic grammar
Although you can make quick progress early on, it is not possible to get to an advanced level in Modern Standard Arabic without having a firm grasp of grammar
Is it really necessary to learn 5,000 words
Studies suggest that you can participate in a conversation on everyday subjects with a vocabulary of 2,000 to 3,000 words, but to read a newspaper with ease an active vocabulary including many of the journalism specific terms and expressions is necessary
